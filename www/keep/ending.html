What is 'End your siRNA with TT/UU/dNdN/NN ' ?

For each 23mer with the your pattern, 21mer sense is the string from positions 3 to 23, whereas the 21mer antisense is the string from reverse complement of positions 1 to 21. The "End" refers to the last 2 nucleotides at the 3'end of both the sense and antisense 21mer.
First, all the 21mers are converted to RNAs and the 2 nucleotides at the 3'end of 21mers are replaced by your choice. If you choose NN, the original 2 nucleotides are kept intact. e.g.:
    If your 23mer is GAAGCAAGAGGATTGCATTCAGC

  1. If you choose "End siRNA with TT":
    2.  21mer sense:     5' AGCAAGAGGAUUGCAUUCAdTdT
 21mer antisense: 5' UGAAUGCAAUCCUCUUGCUdTdT
  2. If you choose "End siRNA with UU":
    3.  21mer sense:     5' AGCAAGAGGAUUGCAUUCAUU
 21mer antisense: 5' UGAAUGCAAUCCUCUUGCUUU
  3. If you choose "End siRNA with dNdN":
    4.  21mer sense:     5' AGCAAGAGGAUUGCAUUCAdGdC 
 21mer antisense: 5' UGAAUGCAAUCCUCUUGCUdUdC
  4. If you choose "End siRNA with NN":
    5.  21mer sense:     5' AGCAAGAGGAUUGCAUUCAGC
 21mer antisense: 5' UGAAUGCAAUCCUCUUGCUUC
